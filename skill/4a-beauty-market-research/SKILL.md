# 4A Beauty Market Research

## Purpose

Turn a broad brand or website-rebuild request into a consulting-grade market research and strategy report. The goal is not to produce generic market commentary; it is to create a decision document that clarifies market opportunity, customer buying logic, channel strategy, competitive position, claim boundaries, and execution priorities.

## Core Workflow

1. Define the brief and success criteria.
   - Restate the brand, product, market, audience, and requested output.
   - Identify whether the task is research-only, strategy, website rebuild input, or campaign planning.
   - Name assumptions explicitly. Ask only for blocking facts; otherwise record missing facts as "brand-side required inputs."

2. Separate researchable facts from brand-side facts.
   - Researchable: market size, regulation, platform policies, competitor products, pricing, claims, channels, social behavior, keyword demand, and public IP/trademark status.
   - Brand-side: product source, formula, INCI, test reports, net content, manufacturing status, batch data, margin, logistics, target launch market, legal review, and commercial constraints.

3. Build an evidence plan before writing conclusions.
   - Prioritize official/regulatory/platform sources, competitor pages, current pricing pages, recent market reports, academic reviews, and credible social/channel evidence.
   - For unstable information such as laws, platform policies, pricing, market size, or trademark status, verify live when browsing is available.
   - Use dates in the report when the fact is time-sensitive.

4. Analyze six layers.
   - Market: TAM/SAM/SOM, growth stage, category maturity, premium pricing capacity, and regional differences.
   - Regulation and platform risk: legal boundaries, advertising policies, medical or therapeutic claim risk, data/privacy issues when relevant.
   - Consumer: core buyers, non-buyers, pains, itch points, delight points, willingness to pay, buying journey, and objections.
   - Channel: SEO, Google, Meta, TikTok, YouTube, Reddit/community, creators, email/SMS, marketplaces, professional/provider channels.
   - Competitors: category map, price table, product claims, evidence level, channel strength, weaknesses, and whitespace.
   - Brand self-analysis: confirmed assets, gaps, SWOT, value loop, differentiation, and trust assets.

5. Convert research into strategy.
   - Positioning sentence.
   - Positioning formula: for whom, what value, why different, what proof.
   - Slogan and message options.
   - Claims red lines and safer replacement language.
   - Product and pricing strategy.
   - Website information architecture.
   - 90/180/365-day execution plan.
   - KPI and unit economics model when commercial assumptions exist.

6. Answer the four strategic questions.
   - Who pays?
   - Why do they pay?
   - Why choose this brand instead of competitors?
   - What evidence supports choosing this brand?

7. Verify and iterate.
   - After drafting, scan for unsupported claims, stale facts, unresolved contradictions, and missing brand-side inputs.
   - If the user supplies new product facts, update the report instead of leaving old "unknown" language in place.

## Source Discipline

Use `references/source-strategy.md` when the task involves current market, legal, platform, pricing, or competitor facts.

Minimum standard:

- Cite or link key sources when final reports need evidence.
- Prefer primary sources for regulation and platform policy.
- Treat market report numbers as directional unless methodology and scope are clear.
- Do not present legal, medical, or regulatory analysis as formal advice.
- When a claim may affect compliance, say what must be reviewed by counsel or a qualified regulatory advisor.

## Report Shape

Use `references/report-template.md` for the full report outline. Tailor it down for lightweight tasks, but keep the four strategic questions and the "researchable vs brand-side facts" boundary.

## Voice

Write like a professional strategy consulting team:

- Direct, specific, and evidence-led.
- Clear about tradeoffs and uncertainties.
- Practical enough to guide website, content, channel, and go-to-market execution.
- Avoid hype, miracle claims, and vague "big market" language.
