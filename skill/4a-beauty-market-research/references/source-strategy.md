# Source Strategy

Use this reference when the report depends on current market, legal, platform, competitor, pricing, or IP facts.

## Source Hierarchy

1. Official sources
   - FDA, FTC, Health Canada, EU, USPTO, EUIPO, customs, privacy regulators.
   - Google Ads, Meta, TikTok, Amazon, Shopify, payment processors, marketplace policy pages.

2. Competitor primary evidence
   - Official product pages, pricing pages, FAQs, claims, clinical/evidence pages, professional program pages, ingredient pages, policy pages.

3. Market and consumer data
   - Recent market reports, retail panels, social platform reports, Pew/DataReportal-style usage reports, credible industry publications.
   - Use directional language when methodology is unavailable or definitions differ.

4. Scientific and technical context
   - Peer-reviewed reviews, clinical studies, safety reviews, ingredient or technology explainers.
   - Do not over-transfer study conclusions to a specific product unless the product itself was tested.

5. Social and community signals
   - Reddit, TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, creator reviews, forums, customer reviews.
   - Treat as qualitative insight, not proof of efficacy.

## Search Patterns

Use combinations like:

```text
[category] market size [country/region] [year]
[ingredient/product type] FDA warning letter official
[ingredient/product type] EU cosmetics regulation Annex official
[brand] [product] price size claims
[brand] professional program skincare
[platform] ads policy healthcare cosmetics before after official
[category] Reddit reviews competitors
[category] TikTok trends skincare [year]
```

## Evidence Notes

For each high-impact fact, capture:

- Source title and URL.
- Publish/update date when visible.
- What the source proves.
- Whether it is primary, secondary, or directional.
- Any mismatch between source scope and the user's product.

## Red Flags

Pause or qualify conclusions when:

- Product source, formula, INCI, net content, or claims are unknown.
- Public pricing differs across retailers.
- Regulatory status is based only on secondary summaries.
- Platform policy is time-sensitive or unclear.
- A claim implies treatment, cure, inflammation reduction, tissue repair, regeneration, disease, injection, or body structure/function change.
